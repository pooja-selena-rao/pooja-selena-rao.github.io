Biography

Dr Pooja Selena Rao is a doctoral researcher at the Centre for Transport Engineering and Modeling within the Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering at Imperial College London. Since 2021, her doctoral research has been supported by the Dixon Scholarship.

Her research investigates the behavioral and policy dimensions of Shared Automated Electric Vehicles (SAEVs), with particular emphasis on understanding the psychological, social and economic factors influencing technology adoption in London. Her work combines behavioral modeling, transport engineering, data analytics and public policy to develop evidence-based recommendations for sustainable urban mobility.

Her research has been presented at international conferences on transport, climate change, environmental sustainability and future smart cities. Her conference proceedings have been published in Springer Nature’s Advances in Science, Technology & Innovation series.

Her recent work, published in Elsevier’s Transportation Research Interdisciplinary Perspectives, develops a hybrid modeling framework for analyzing community-wide adoption of Shared Automated Electric Vehicles and evaluating policy interventions that encourage sustainable transport transitions.

Research Vision

My research aims to understand how behavioral science, engineering and public policy can work together to accelerate the adoption of sustainable transport technologies. By combining quantitative modeling with behavioral insights, I seek to develop evidence-based policies that support the transition to smarter, cleaner and more equitable mobility systems.

Previous Research

Prior to her doctoral research, Dr Rao worked across quantitative finance, behavioral finance and financial engineering. Her earlier research examined behavioral influences on portfolio allocation, multivariate GARCH models for financial market volatility, and cryptocurrency market microstructure, including blockchain ownership dynamics and Bitcoin exchange behavior.
